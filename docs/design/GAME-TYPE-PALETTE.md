# Game-type palette & anatomy — a working reference for curation

A working sheet to curate the game-type list from. Two parts: **what a game type
*is*** (the categorical statement + the six axes you fill per type), and a broad
**palette** of candidate types (by engagement shape) plus the orthogonal
genre/setting axis. Cut, rename, merge, reorder freely — this is raw material.

---

## Part 1 — What a game type consists of

**Categorical statement:**
> *A game type is a claim about the **shape of engagement** — the core loop the
> player is really playing, and therefore what the narrator **dramatizes vs.
> hand-waves** to serve that loop, how **tension** builds, and what **winning and
> losing** mean — valid only for a world whose substance can **support** that loop.*

A type is these six axes filled in:

| # | Axis | The question it answers | Feeds |
|---|------|-------------------------|-------|
| 1 | **Core loop** | What is the player actually *doing*, turn to turn, that *is* the fun? | the directive |
| 2 | **Dramatize** | What does the narrator slow down and render in rich detail? | the directive |
| 3 | **Hand-wave** | What compresses to a sentence (connective tissue)? | the directive |
| 4 | **Tension shape** | How does pressure build — ratchet / accrete / grind / warm? | the directive |
| 5 | **Win / loss shape** | What does victory *mean*; how does it go wrong? | the arc author's win/loss prior |
| 6 | **World requires** | What must the world *contain* for this loop to run at all? | the ingest "can this fiction support it?" check |

- Axes **1–4** distill into the **maintained directive** (the paragraph that rides
  in the narrator briefing every turn).
- Axis **5** is the **win/loss prior** the arc author builds toward.
- Axis **6** is the **support-check** (the Hobbit-can't-be-a-dating-sim gate,
  formalized): if you can't fill axis 6 against a given fiction, the type doesn't fit.

**Worked example — Mystery:**
- **Core loop:** gather information and reason toward a hidden truth.
- **Dramatize:** clues, testimony, scenes of evidence, contradictions between accounts.
- **Hand-wave:** travel, logistics, passage between locations.
- **Tension shape:** accretion — the noose tightens as facts align and suspects squirm.
- **Win / loss:** name the truth / right accusation · vs. a wrong accusation or cold case.
- **World requires:** a concealed truth, ≥2 suspects with motives, discoverable
  evidence, red herrings.

→ which collapses into a one-paragraph directive.

**Authoring template (copy one block per type):**
```

- Core loop:
- Dramatize:
- Hand-wave:
- Tension shape:
- Win / loss:
- World requires:
```

## Part 2 — Candidate palette (by engagement shape)

Drawn from PnP traditions (D&D, Call of Cthulhu, Blades in the Dark, Vampire,
Pendragon…), film/TV, and fiction. The **engagement shape** is the curatable axis;
genre/setting (below) is a separate flavor layer that pairs with any of these.

### Investigation & truth-seeking
- **Mystery / Whodunnit** — sift clues and testimony toward a hidden truth.
- **Detective Noir** — same, morally grey, the corrupt city as a character.
- **Paranormal / Occult Investigation** — uncover the unknowable; sanity & dread at stake.
- **Conspiracy / Procedural** — peel back a layered cover-up, step by step.
- **Courtroom / Trial** — assemble and argue a case to a verdict.

### Schemes, infiltration & deception
- **Heist / Caper** — execute an intricate multi-stage plan under rising pressure.
- **Espionage / Spy** — tradecraft, cover identities, double-crosses.
- **Escape / Prison-break** — chain narrative puzzles toward getting out.
- **Con / Grift** — work the mark, maintain the lie, manage exposure.

### Action, combat & adventure
- **Combat Adventure / Heroic Quest** — confront threats toward a goal (slay the dragon).
- **Dungeon Crawl** — room-by-room delve; risk vs. reward.
- **Monster Hunt** — track and bring down the beast.
- **War / Military** — campaigns, command, the cost of battle.
- **Action Thriller** — relentless momentum and ticking clocks.

### Survival & endurance
- **Survival** — scarcity, exposure, attrition; every choice costs.
- **Survival Horror** — endure a predatory threat with dwindling resources.
- **Disaster** — get out of a catastrophe alive.
- **Post-Apocalyptic** — endure and rebuild in the ruins.

### Exploration & discovery
- **Exploration / Adventure** — discovery, wonder, traversal.
- **Sandbox / Hexcrawl** — open-ended wandering; goals emerge.
- **Weird / Mystery-of-Place** — uncover what a place or phenomenon *is*.

### Social, relationship & court
- **Romance / Dating-sim** — cultivate a relationship to its turning point.
- **Court Intrigue / Political** — alliances, leverage, betrayal, power.
- **Drama / Slice-of-Life** — character and small stakes over plot.
- **Coming-of-Age** — growth, identity, formative choices.

### Horror & dread
- **Gothic Horror** — decay, the haunted, creeping dread.
- **Cosmic Horror** — the unknowable, futility, sanity erosion.
- **Slasher** — a predator, dwindling survivors, mounting panic.

### Tonal / structural lenses (modifiers, probably *not* types)
- Comedy / Farce · Tragedy · Revenge · Redemption · Underdog / Sports · Picaresque.

---

## The orthogonal axis — genre / setting (flavor, not loop)
Pairs with *any* type above: **high fantasy · sword-&-sorcery · sci-fi / space opera
· cyberpunk · post-apocalyptic · historical · modern / contemporary · urban fantasy
· western · steampunk · mythic / folklore · superhero.**
(e.g. *fantasy* genre × *combat* type = slay the dragon; *fantasy* genre × *romance*
type = win the maiden — same world-genre, different game.)

---

## Curation notes (from the weeds)
- **Strongest, most distinct** (they almost write their own directives): Mystery ·
  Heist/Escape · Romance · Survival · Horror-Investigation · Combat-Adventure ·
  Court-Intrigue · Exploration.
- **Near-variants worth merging** to keep the list tight: Noir ⊂ Mystery; Slasher ⊂
  Survival-Horror; Conspiracy ⊂ Mystery.
- **Tonal lenses** (comedy, tragedy, revenge) are modifiers, not resolution lenses —
  better as a tone tag than a game type.
- The **win/loss prior** is the payoff: type largely determines the destination, so
  it's chosen at session-zero right before the ending question.
