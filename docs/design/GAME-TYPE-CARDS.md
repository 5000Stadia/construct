# Game-Type Cards

A game type is a claim about the **shape of engagement** — the core loop the player is
actually playing, and therefore what the story should **dramatize vs. hand-wave**, how
**tension** builds, and what **winning and losing** mean. It is orthogonal to genre
(fantasy/sci-fi/noir) and to tone (comedy/tragedy/grim). Any type here pairs with many
genres; the test of a real type is that it changes *what the narrator dwells on vs. skips*
and *what victory means*. Cards are grouped by family.

---

## Family: Investigation

### Mystery / Whodunnit
- **Name:** Mystery / Whodunnit
- **One-line pitch:** You sift clues and conflicting testimony to expose a hidden truth.
- **Core loop:** gather information and reason toward a concealed answer.
- **Dramatize:** clues, witness testimony, scenes of evidence, the contradictions between accounts; the player weighing motive, history, and red herrings.
- **Hand-wave:** travel, logistics, the passage between locations — getting somewhere is never the game.
- **Tension shape:** accretion — the noose tightens as facts align and suspects squirm.
- **Win condition:** name the truth / make the correct accusation, supported by what was actually uncovered.
- **Loss condition:** a wrong accusation, or the trail goes cold and the case is lost.
- **World requires:** a concealed truth; ≥2 suspects with plausible motives; discoverable evidence; at least one red herring.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — MYSTERY: Compress travel and logistics to a sentence — getting somewhere is never the game. Slow down and give deep clarity to clues, testimony, scenes of evidence, and the contradictions between witnesses' stories; let the player sift motive, history, and red herrings toward the truth. Withhold the answer and reward deduction.`

### Investigative Procedural
- **Name:** Investigative Procedural
- **One-line pitch:** You work a case the slow institutional way — interviews, records, forensics, warrants — until it holds up.
- **Core loop:** accumulate and cross-check evidence through method and process; build a case that can survive scrutiny.
- **Dramatize:** procedure and its friction — interrogation tactics, chain of custody, lab results, paperwork that gates the next move; the grind of one lead opening the next.
- **Hand-wave:** the broad geography and downtime; emotional interiority unless it cracks a witness.
- **Tension shape:** grind/accretion — a ratchet of confirmed facts against a closing window (a suspect about to flee, a clock on detention).
- **Win condition:** a case that holds — enough corroborated evidence to convict/close, the right perpetrator identified by method.
- **Loss condition:** the case collapses (tainted evidence, blown procedure), the suspect walks, or the window closes.
- **World requires:** institutional machinery (records, labs, authority to compel); a perpetrator and a method; procedural gates that can be passed or failed.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — INVESTIGATIVE PROCEDURAL: Render procedure in detail — interrogations, records, forensics, the paperwork and warrants that gate each step — and make each confirmed fact unlock the next. Compress travel and downtime. Tension is a case that must hold up under scrutiny against a closing window; a blown procedure costs the player, not just a wrong guess.`

### Conspiracy / Paranoia
- **Name:** Conspiracy / Paranoia
- **One-line pitch:** You pull a thread and the pattern keeps getting bigger and no one can be trusted.
- **Core loop:** connect scattered, ambiguous signals into a hidden structure while judging who to trust.
- **Dramatize:** the act of connection — pinboard logic, the half-meaning of a coincidence, surveillance and being surveilled, the recalibration of trust after each revelation.
- **Hand-wave:** mundane life between contacts; the ordinary world the player is sliding away from.
- **Tension shape:** escalating dread + tightening net — each answer enlarges the threat and shrinks the circle of allies.
- **Win condition:** expose or escape the conspiracy with proof that survives, OR correctly map the structure and act on it before it acts on you.
- **Loss condition:** trust the wrong person, get neutralized/discredited, or assemble a false pattern (paranoia wins instead of truth).
- **World requires:** a real hidden structure (not just vibes); multiple agents with concealed allegiances; ambiguous evidence that genuinely supports more than one reading; stakes that reach the player personally.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — CONSPIRACY: Dramatize the connecting of dots and the constant re-weighing of who can be trusted; let each revelation make the threat bigger and the player more alone. Keep evidence genuinely ambiguous so a false pattern is always possible. Skip ordinary life. Punish misplaced trust as hard as a wrong deduction.`

### Trial / Courtroom
- **Name:** Trial / Courtroom
- **One-line pitch:** The facts are mostly known; the fight is over what they mean in front of a judge.
- **Core loop:** marshal evidence and testimony into argument, attack the opposing case, and persuade an arbiter under rules.
- **Dramatize:** examination and cross-examination, objections, the turning of a witness, the precise wording of an argument, the arbiter's reactions.
- **Hand-wave:** the underlying investigation (often already done); life outside the proceeding.
- **Tension shape:** episodic spikes inside a rising arc — each witness/round is a contest, building to verdict.
- **Win condition:** the verdict goes your way — persuade the arbiter, break the opposing case.
- **Loss condition:** lose the verdict, get a key argument struck, or a witness destroys your position.
- **World requires:** a rule-bound forum and an arbiter; two contestable readings of the facts; witnesses/evidence that can be examined and impeached; defined win = the judgment.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — TRIAL: Slow down on argument and cross-examination — objections, the exact phrasing of a question, the moment a witness cracks, the arbiter's tells. The fight is over meaning, not facts; hand-wave the prior investigation. Build round by round toward a verdict the player can win or lose.`

## Family: Schemes & Infiltration

### Heist
- **Name:** Heist
- **One-line pitch:** You case a hard target, assemble a plan, and execute it as it falls apart in interesting ways.
- **Core loop:** gather intel on a defended target, prepare assets and a plan, then execute and improvise around contact with reality.
- **Dramatize:** the score's defenses and their seams, the assembling of crew/tools, the execution beat-by-beat, and the improvisation when the plan meets friction.
- **Hand-wave:** ordinary downtime, travel; the spoils after the getaway (the *having* is not the game, the *taking* is).
- **Tension shape:** ratchet to a climax — a long quiet load, then a tightening execution where complications spike.
- **Win condition:** secure the objective and get clear (the score, and the exit).
- **Loss condition:** caught, the score lost, or an unrecoverable blown step during execution.
- **World requires:** a defended objective worth taking; legible defenses with exploitable weaknesses; means to prepare (intel, tools, crew); a viable but non-trivial exit.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — HEIST: Render the target's defenses and their seams, the prep of crew and tools, and above all the execution beat-by-beat — then throw friction at the plan and dramatize the improvisation. Hand-wave downtime and the spoils. Tension is a quiet build into a tightening run for the exit; getting clear is half the win.`

### Infiltration / Stealth
- **Name:** Infiltration / Stealth
- **One-line pitch:** You move through hostile space unseen, where being noticed is the failure.
- **Core loop:** read patrol patterns and sightlines, choose moments and routes, and avoid or quietly neutralize detection.
- **Dramatize:** position, timing, sightlines and sound, the state of guards and alarms, the held breath of a near-miss.
- **Hand-wave:** the wider world; anything outside the secured perimeter and the clock.
- **Tension shape:** accrete/tighten — a rising alert state; small mistakes compound toward exposure.
- **Win condition:** reach the objective and exfiltrate without raising (or while surviving) the alarm.
- **Loss condition:** detection escalates to lockdown/capture, or the objective becomes unreachable.
- **World requires:** a guarded space with patterns; detection mechanics (sight, sound, alarm states); an objective inside; an escape path.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — INFILTRATION: Track position, timing, sightlines, sound, and alert state with precision — every guard's gaze and every alarm's threshold matters. Make detection the real enemy and a near-miss the core thrill. Skip the outside world. Let small slips compound toward exposure.`

### Con / Grift
- **Name:** Con / Grift
- **One-line pitch:** You build a false reality in a mark's head and steer them into walking off the cliff themselves.
- **Core loop:** establish a persona and a convincing pretext, manage what the mark believes, and protect the illusion until the take.
- **Dramatize:** the performance — reading the mark, the improvised cover story, managing leaks and suspicion, the social tightrope of staying in character.
- **Hand-wave:** logistics of the setup; physical action (the con avoids force by definition).
- **Tension shape:** slow warming with spike — confidence accretes, suspicion threatens, then the sting and the fade.
- **Win condition:** the mark acts as desired and you exit before the illusion collapses (the take + the clean fade).
- **Loss condition:** the cover cracks, the mark wises up, or you're caught inside the lie.
- **World requires:** a mark with something to take and beliefs that can be shaped; a persona the player can sustain; ways the lie can leak; an exit before exposure.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — CON: Dramatize the performance — reading the mark, holding a persona, improvising cover, and patching leaks before suspicion sets. The game is the managed illusion, not force; hand-wave logistics. Build confidence slowly, threaten it constantly, and make the clean fade as important as the take.`

### Political Maneuvering
- **Name:** Political Maneuvering
- **One-line pitch:** You climb and survive a web of power by trading favors, leverage, and loyalty.
- **Core loop:** read the board of factions and players, accumulate leverage and alliances, and spend them to shift power your way.
- **Dramatize:** negotiations, the subtext of a conversation, shifting alliances and betrayals, the calculus of debts owed and held, reputation and standing.
- **Hand-wave:** physical action and travel; the mechanics of governance itself (the *struggle* for power, not its administration).
- **Tension shape:** accrete/positional — leverage banks and erodes; betrayals are episodic spikes within a long ascent or defense.
- **Win condition:** reach/hold the position of power you sought, your key alliances intact.
- **Loss condition:** outmaneuvered, betrayed into ruin, or your leverage spent for nothing.
- **World requires:** multiple factions/players with distinct interests; currencies of power (favors, secrets, titles, force-in-reserve); a contested position; relationships that can shift.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — POLITICAL: Slow down on negotiation, subtext, alliances, debts, and reputation — every conversation is a move and every favor a banked asset. Hand-wave physical action and the dull machinery of governance. Let leverage accrue and erode, and make betrayal a live possibility at every step toward the seat of power.`

### Espionage / Spycraft
- **Name:** Espionage / Spycraft
- **One-line pitch:** You run sources, cover, and tradecraft in hostile territory where one slip burns everything.
- **Core loop:** maintain cover, cultivate and run assets, exchange information, and manage exposure against counter-intelligence.
- **Dramatize:** tradecraft (dead drops, cover stories, surveillance detection), the management of trust with assets, the cold calculus of burning vs. protecting a source.
- **Hand-wave:** ordinary life; bulk travel — except where a border crossing IS the risk.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming + cliff — long quiet maintenance under a rising risk of being made, punctuated by exposure spikes.
- **Win condition:** secure the intelligence/objective and protect your network and cover.
- **Loss condition:** blown cover, a burned/turned asset, capture, or feeding the enemy what they wanted.
- **World requires:** opposing intelligence interests; cover that can hold or crack; assets with their own motives and loyalties; consequences for exposure that reach the network.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — ESPIONAGE: Dramatize tradecraft and the running of human sources — cover stories, dead drops, surveillance detection, and the cold choice of whether to burn an asset. Maintain a slow, quiet build of exposure risk that can spike to a burn. Hand-wave ordinary life. A blown cover or a turned source costs the whole network.`

## Family: Action & Combat

### Tactical Combat
- **Name:** Tactical Combat
- **One-line pitch:** You win fights by position, timing, resources, and exploiting the battlefield.
- **Core loop:** assess a combat situation, commit to tactics (position, ability, resource), and adapt as the engagement evolves.
- **Dramatize:** the geometry and state of the fight — positioning, cover, threats, abilities and their costs, the consequence of each exchange.
- **Hand-wave:** travel between fights, downtime; non-combat narrative compresses to setup and aftermath.
- **Tension shape:** episodic spikes — discrete encounters, each a pressure curve, escalating in difficulty across the arc.
- **Win condition:** survive/win the decisive engagement and achieve its objective.
- **Loss condition:** defeat in a fight that matters — death, capture, or failure of the encounter's stake.
- **World requires:** opposition that can fight; a combat space with tactical features; the player has actionable capabilities and resources; encounters with real stakes.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — TACTICAL COMBAT: Slow down inside fights — render position, cover, threats, abilities, and the cost of each exchange, and let the situation shift as the player commits. Compress travel and downtime to setup and aftermath. Make encounters discrete pressure spikes that escalate, with real stakes on the decisive ones.`

### Action / Thriller
- **Name:** Action / Thriller
- **One-line pitch:** A relentless chain of set-pieces where momentum never lets you stop to think.
- **Core loop:** react fast to escalating threats, keep moving, and convert nerve and improvisation into survival and progress.
- **Dramatize:** kinetic set-pieces — chases, fights, narrow escapes, deadlines — rendered for momentum and stakes, not granular tactics.
- **Hand-wave:** quiet reflection, logistics, anything that kills pace; recovery is brief.
- **Tension shape:** ratchet of escalating set-pieces toward a climactic confrontation; rarely lets the throttle fully off.
- **Win condition:** reach and survive the climactic confrontation, objective achieved.
- **Loss condition:** caught by the threat, fail a deadline, or fall in a set-piece you can't escape.
- **World requires:** a persistent escalating threat/antagonist; a sequence of obstacles that can be set-pieces; a deadline or pursuit; an achievable climax.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — ACTION/THRILLER: Keep the throttle down — chases, fights, narrow escapes, deadlines — rendered for momentum and stakes rather than granular tactics. Hand-wave reflection and logistics; never let the player stop to think for long. Escalate set-piece to set-piece toward a single climactic confrontation.`

### Duel / Showdown
- **Name:** Duel / Showdown
- **One-line pitch:** Everything narrows to one decisive confrontation with a worthy adversary.
- **Core loop:** read your opponent, manage tells and openings, and commit to the decisive exchange.
- **Dramatize:** the charged stillness before, the read of the adversary, the micro-beats of the exchange, the meaning riding on the outcome.
- **Hand-wave:** everything that isn't the confrontation and its immediate frame; minor opposition.
- **Tension shape:** compression to a single point — slow taut build, then a sudden decisive resolution.
- **Win condition:** prevail in the decisive exchange (which may be martial, verbal, or willpower).
- **Loss condition:** lose the exchange — and with it whatever it staked.
- **World requires:** a singular adversary of comparable weight; a confrontation that carries decisive stakes; a frame that legitimizes a one-on-one resolution.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — DUEL: Compress everything toward one confrontation with a worthy adversary; dramatize the charged build, the reading of tells and openings, and the micro-beats of the decisive exchange. Hand-wave all lesser opposition. Let a single outcome carry the full weight of the stakes.`

### War / Command
- **Name:** War / Command
- **One-line pitch:** You direct forces and resources across a campaign where individual fights are abstracted into outcomes.
- **Core loop:** make strategic and operational decisions — allocate forces, choose objectives, weigh intelligence and logistics — and live with the consequences.
- **Dramatize:** the decision space — intelligence, terrain, supply, morale, the cost-benefit of an offensive; the human weight of orders given.
- **Hand-wave:** individual combat (resolved as outcomes, not blow-by-blow); personal logistics.
- **Tension shape:** grind/attrition across a campaign with episodic decisive battles.
- **Win condition:** achieve the campaign objective — win the war, hold the line, secure the strategic aim.
- **Loss condition:** strategic defeat, your force broken, the objective lost.
- **World requires:** forces and resources to command; a theater with terrain/supply/objectives; an opposing strategic actor; consequences that scale beyond one fight.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — WAR/COMMAND: Dramatize the decision space — intelligence, terrain, supply, morale, the cost of an order — and resolve individual battles as outcomes, not blow-by-blow. The player commands; make them feel the weight of choices that cost lives and ground. Build through attrition toward decisive engagements and a campaign verdict.`

## Family: Survival

### Survival / Endurance
- **Name:** Survival / Endurance
- **One-line pitch:** You ration scarce resources against a hostile environment that grinds you down.
- **Core loop:** manage needs and resources (food, warmth, health, tools) against environmental and time pressure; every choice trades one scarcity for another.
- **Dramatize:** scarcity and its math — the state of supplies, the body's needs, the environment's threats, the cost of every expenditure.
- **Hand-wave:** rich social texture and worldbuilding beyond what bears on survival; uneventful safe stretches.
- **Tension shape:** grind/attrition — slow tightening as reserves dwindle, with acute crises.
- **Win condition:** endure to rescue/escape/stabilization, or build a sustainable foothold.
- **Loss condition:** a need goes critical and fatal — starvation, exposure, injury, the environment wins.
- **World requires:** genuine scarcity; an environment with ongoing threats; tracked needs/resources with depletion; a possible end-state (rescue, escape, sustainability).
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — SURVIVAL: Render scarcity as math the player feels — supplies, the body's needs, the environment's threats, the cost of every expenditure where each choice trades one shortage for another. Hand-wave safe, uneventful stretches and texture that doesn't bear on staying alive. Grind reserves down with crises until rescue, escape, or a sustainable foothold.`

### Disaster / Escape
- **Name:** Disaster / Escape
- **One-line pitch:** A catastrophe is unfolding and you must get out before it closes over you.
- **Core loop:** read a collapsing situation, find and commit to a route out, and outrun an advancing threat under time pressure.
- **Dramatize:** the advancing catastrophe and the shrinking options — the route, the obstacle, the choice of who/what to save, the closing window.
- **Hand-wave:** backstory and aftermath; anything outside the disaster's immediate radius.
- **Tension shape:** ratchet against a hard clock — the threat advances regardless; pressure only rises.
- **Win condition:** escape the disaster zone (with what/whom you chose to save).
- **Loss condition:** the catastrophe overtakes you — trapped, too slow, wrong route.
- **World requires:** an advancing, escalating hazard; a constrained escape with real obstacles; a clock; meaningful trade-offs about what to leave behind.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — DISASTER/ESCAPE: Make the catastrophe advance on its own clock and the options shrink — dramatize the route, the obstacle, the closing window, and the brutal choice of what to leave behind. Hand-wave backstory and aftermath. Pressure only rises; the threat does not wait for the player.`

### Base / Settlement Building
- **Name:** Base / Settlement Building
- **One-line pitch:** You wrest a foothold from a hostile world and grow it against pressures that want it gone.
- **Core loop:** secure resources, build and improve, manage a community/system, and defend it against recurring threats.
- **Dramatize:** the state and growth of the foothold — what's built, what's needed next, the standing of its people/systems, the looming threats it must withstand.
- **Hand-wave:** moment-to-moment travel and minor labor; flavor that doesn't change the settlement's state.
- **Tension shape:** accrete with episodic siege/crisis spikes — long building punctuated by threats to what you've built.
- **Win condition:** a self-sustaining, defensible settlement that meets its goal (survive the winter, reach independence, etc.).
- **Loss condition:** the foothold collapses — overrun, starved, fractured from within.
- **World requires:** a buildable foothold; resources to gather and convert; a community or systems with needs; recurring external/internal threats; a defined sustainability goal.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — SETTLEMENT: Track the foothold's state and growth in detail — what's built, what's needed, who's restless, what threat looms — and make each investment a visible step. Hand-wave minor labor and travel. Build long stretches, then test the settlement with sieges and crises; victory is something that can stand on its own.`

## Family: Exploration

### Exploration / Discovery
- **Name:** Exploration / Discovery
- **One-line pitch:** You push into the unknown and the world rewards you for looking.
- **Core loop:** venture into unmapped space, read environmental cues, and uncover places, secrets, and connections.
- **Dramatize:** place and wonder — vistas, the texture of the unknown, the payoff of a discovery, the slow assembly of a mental map.
- **Hand-wave:** repetitive backtracking through known space; combat (resolved quickly unless it gates a discovery).
- **Tension shape:** slow warming with discovery spikes — curiosity pulls forward; danger rises with depth.
- **Win condition:** reach/map the goal, find the hidden thing, complete the map of the unknown.
- **Loss condition:** lost beyond return, the goal unreachable, or undone by a hazard you walked into.
- **World requires:** unmapped space worth crossing; discoverable features/secrets keyed to looking; environmental cues that reward attention; a goal at the edge of the known.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — EXPLORATION: Slow down on place and wonder — vistas, the texture of the unknown, the payoff of finding something, the map assembling in the player's head. Reward curiosity and reading the environment. Hand-wave backtracking and incidental combat. Let danger deepen with distance from the known.`

### Dungeon Crawl
- **Name:** Dungeon Crawl
- **One-line pitch:** You descend through a dangerous, room-by-room space, spending resources to go deeper.
- **Core loop:** advance through discrete dangerous spaces, manage resources and risk, and decide how deep to push before retreating.
- **Dramatize:** the room-by-room reveal — traps, threats, loot, the choice to press on or pull back, the dwindling of resources against the lure of going deeper.
- **Hand-wave:** the surface world; travel to and from; narrative outside the descent.
- **Tension shape:** accrete/attrition with depth — resources drain, danger rises, the push-your-luck curve sharpens.
- **Win condition:** reach the depth/objective and (usually) get back out with the prize.
- **Loss condition:** resources run out at depth, a fatal encounter, or being trapped below.
- **World requires:** a bounded dangerous space with discrete rooms/levels; threats, traps, and rewards keyed to depth; depletable resources; a deep objective and a way back.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — DUNGEON CRAWL: Reveal the space room by room — traps, threats, loot — and frame every advance as a push-your-luck bet against dwindling resources. Dramatize the choice to press deeper or pull back. Hand-wave the surface world. Let danger and reward both rise with depth.`

### Journey / Pilgrimage
- **Name:** Journey / Pilgrimage
- **One-line pitch:** The road is the story; you and your companions change by traveling it.
- **Core loop:** progress along a long route through episodic encounters, managing the party and being changed by what's met.
- **Dramatize:** the road's encounters and the people on it — the way a place or a meeting marks the travelers, the slow shift in relationships and self.
- **Hand-wave:** uneventful miles (compressed to mood and passage); precise logistics unless scarcity bites.
- **Tension shape:** episodic spikes along a slow warming arc toward arrival/transformation.
- **Win condition:** arrive — reach the destination, and/or complete the inner change the journey was for.
- **Loss condition:** the party breaks, the journey is abandoned, or you arrive having lost what mattered.
- **World requires:** a meaningful destination and a long route; episodic encounters with their own texture; companions/self that can be changed; a reason the going matters.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — JOURNEY: Make the road the story — dramatize its encounters and the people on it, and how each marks the travelers and shifts their bonds. Compress uneventful miles into mood and passage. Build episodically toward an arrival that is as much inner change as a place reached.`

### Survival Sandbox / Open World
- **Name:** Open-World Sandbox
- **One-line pitch:** You set your own goals in a living world and chase whatever pulls you.
- **Core loop:** roam a persistent world, surface opportunities, choose what to pursue, and shape your own throughline.
- **Dramatize:** the living world's reactions and opportunities — what's happening, what's possible, the consequences of the player's self-chosen pursuits.
- **Hand-wave:** any thread the player isn't pursuing (kept warm but not rendered); fast travel through known space.
- **Tension shape:** player-driven; emergent spikes from chosen goals rather than an authored ramp.
- **Win condition:** self-defined — the player's chosen goal achieved (no single mandated victory).
- **Loss condition:** self-defined failure, or a world consequence the player can't recover from.
- **World requires:** a persistent reactive world; many simultaneous threads/opportunities; freedom to choose direction; consequences that persist and propagate.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — OPEN WORLD: Surface a living world full of opportunities and let the player choose the throughline; render what they pursue and keep the rest warm but compressed. Make the world react and remember. Tension is emergent from the player's own goals — don't impose a single victory.`

## Family: Social & Relationship

### Romance / Courtship
- **Name:** Romance / Courtship
- **One-line pitch:** You navigate the growth (or rupture) of an intimate bond.
- **Core loop:** read and respond to another's emotional state, make choices that build or strain intimacy, and steer the relationship.
- **Dramatize:** emotional beats and subtext — the loaded silence, the vulnerable disclosure, the read of the other's feelings, the turning points of trust and desire.
- **Hand-wave:** plot machinery, action, and logistics except as backdrop that pressures the bond.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming with emotional spikes — closeness and distance oscillate toward a defining moment.
- **Win condition:** the bond reaches a chosen, mutual state (union, reconciliation, an honest understanding).
- **Loss condition:** the bond ruptures or curdles — betrayal, drift, the wrong word at the wrong time.
- **World requires:** at least one other with an inner life and an evolving disposition; intimacy that can grow and break; choices that move the emotional state; a stake the relationship can resolve toward.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — ROMANCE: Slow down on emotional beats and subtext — loaded silences, vulnerable disclosures, the read of the other's feelings, the turning points of trust and desire. Hand-wave plot and action except where they pressure the bond. Oscillate closeness and distance toward a defining moment the player can win or lose.`

### Social Climbing / Status
- **Name:** Social Climbing / Status
- **One-line pitch:** You read and game a social hierarchy to rise within it without being exposed.
- **Core loop:** read social codes and standing, perform the right self, manage reputation, and convert relationships into status.
- **Dramatize:** social performance and reputation — the rules of the milieu, the faux pas and the coup, who's watching, the gap between who you are and who you're pretending to be.
- **Hand-wave:** physical action; anything outside the social arena.
- **Tension shape:** accrete/positional with exposure spikes — standing banks and erodes; the risk of being found out rises with the climb.
- **Win condition:** secure the standing/acceptance sought, your position consolidated.
- **Loss condition:** exposed, snubbed into ruin, or socially destroyed.
- **World requires:** a legible social hierarchy with codes; reputation that can rise and fall; gatekeepers and rivals; a top worth reaching and a way to be exposed.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — SOCIAL CLIMBING: Dramatize social performance and reputation — the codes of the milieu, the faux pas and the coup, who is watching, the gap between the real self and the performed one. Hand-wave physical action. Let standing bank and erode, and raise the risk of exposure with every rung climbed.`

### Mentorship / Bond-Forging
- **Name:** Mentorship / Bond-Forging
- **One-line pitch:** You build a small group of trusted bonds and carry them through what tests them.
- **Core loop:** invest in individual relationships, learn what each needs, and convert trust into loyalty that holds under pressure.
- **Dramatize:** one-on-one moments — the conversation that deepens a bond, the conflict that tests it, the history accruing between people; the standing of each relationship.
- **Hand-wave:** large-scale plot; logistics; the world beyond the circle of bonds.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming with crisis tests — bonds deepen over time, then a pressure event proves or breaks them.
- **Win condition:** the bonds hold and pay off when it counts — loyalty proven, the group intact through the test.
- **Loss condition:** a bond breaks at the crucial moment — betrayal, abandonment, trust that wasn't really earned.
- **World requires:** a set of others with distinct needs and inner lives; relationships that deepen through investment; tests that put loyalty on the line; consequences that depend on the bonds' strength.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — BOND-FORGING: Slow down on one-on-one moments — the conversation that deepens a bond, the conflict that tests it, the history accruing between people. Hand-wave large-scale plot and the world beyond the circle. Let bonds deepen over time, then prove or break them when a crisis demands the loyalty was real.`

### Negotiation / Dealmaking
- **Name:** Negotiation / Dealmaking
- **One-line pitch:** You bargain across a table where information, leverage, and patience decide the split.
- **Core loop:** read the other party's interests and limits, deploy leverage and concession, and close a deal on favorable terms.
- **Dramatize:** the bargaining itself — offers and counters, the bluff and the tell, the discovery of what the other side actually wants, the walk-away threat.
- **Hand-wave:** everything outside the negotiation frame; how the leverage was acquired (often pre-game).
- **Tension shape:** taut oscillation toward a close — pressure builds across rounds, with the deadline/walk-away as the spike.
- **Win condition:** a deal closed on terms you'll take (or a justified, advantageous walk-away).
- **Loss condition:** a bad deal accepted, a good one blown, or the other side walking when you needed it.
- **World requires:** two+ parties with conflicting but overlapping interests; leverage and information asymmetry; a deal that can be struck or broken; real stakes on the terms.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — NEGOTIATION: Dramatize the bargaining — offers and counters, bluff and tell, the slow discovery of what the other side truly wants, the weight of a walk-away. Hand-wave everything outside the table and how leverage was acquired. Build tautly across rounds toward a close the player can win, lose, or rightly walk from.`

### Community / Slice-of-Life
- **Name:** Community / Slice-of-Life
- **One-line pitch:** You live among people whose small troubles and joys, tended over time, become the story.
- **Core loop:** attend to the ordinary lives around you, respond to small needs and frictions, and accrue a web of small consequences.
- **Dramatize:** the texture of daily life and its people — small kindnesses and slights, routines, the slow weather of a community's mood and the player's place in it.
- **Hand-wave:** high-stakes plot, action, grand danger; the world beyond the community.
- **Tension shape:** low-amplitude accretion — gentle pressures and small dilemmas accumulate; rare modest crises.
- **Win condition:** the community/relationships reach a warm, settled state the player tended toward (no grand victory).
- **Loss condition:** the web frays — alienation, a community soured, relationships let go.
- **World requires:** a stable community of distinct people with ordinary lives; small recurring needs and frictions; relationships that respond to attention; persistence so small acts accumulate.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — SLICE-OF-LIFE: Slow down on the texture of daily life — small kindnesses and slights, routines, the slow weather of a community's mood and the player's place in it. Hand-wave grand danger and high-stakes plot. Keep the amplitude low; let small acts accumulate into the player's standing in a web of ordinary lives.`

## Family: Horror

### Survival Horror
- **Name:** Survival Horror
- **One-line pitch:** You endure a predatory threat with too few resources and nowhere safe for long.
- **Core loop:** avoid or fend off a threat you usually can't beat, ration scarce means, and survive toward escape or resolution.
- **Dramatize:** dread and vulnerability — the threat's proximity, the scarcity of safety and supplies, the cost of every noise and choice, the body's fear.
- **Hand-wave:** safe, calm exposition; anything that dispels dread or grants easy power.
- **Tension shape:** oscillating dread — lulls and predatory spikes; overall attrition of safety and resources.
- **Win condition:** survive to escape/resolve the threat (endure, not dominate).
- **Loss condition:** the threat catches you, or attrition leaves you defenseless when it comes.
- **World requires:** a threat stronger than the player; genuine scarcity (safety, light, supplies, ammo); spaces that can be unsafe; a possible escape/resolution.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — SURVIVAL HORROR: Render dread and vulnerability — the threat's proximity, the scarcity of safety and supplies, the cost of every noise. The player endures, not dominates; keep them under-resourced and never safe for long. Hand-wave anything that dispels dread or grants easy power. Oscillate lulls and predatory spikes while safety bleeds away.`

### Cosmic / Sanity Horror
- **Name:** Cosmic / Sanity Horror
- **One-line pitch:** You investigate something that should not be known, and knowing it unmakes you.
- **Core loop:** pursue forbidden understanding while the very act of comprehension erodes the self.
- **Dramatize:** the wrongness — the seep of the uncanny, the cost of each revelation on the mind, the inversion where knowledge is the wound; sanity/grasp as a tracked, failing resource.
- **Hand-wave:** clean victory and reassurance; mundane action that isn't haunted.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming dread toward inevitability — understanding accretes and so does damage; the climax is comprehension, not triumph.
- **Win condition:** a costly, partial victory — survive with mind/soul intact enough, contain rather than conquer; "winning" is often merely enduring the truth.
- **Loss condition:** madness, corruption, or being consumed by what you sought to understand.
- **World requires:** a truth too large/wrong to safely know; a tracked cost-of-knowing (sanity, corruption); the impossibility of clean triumph; revelations that damage as they enlighten.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — COSMIC HORROR: Dramatize the wrongness and the cost of knowing — make each revelation enlarge the threat and erode the self, with sanity or grasp a failing resource. Deny clean victory; the climax is comprehension, not triumph. Hand-wave reassurance. The truth wounds as it illuminates, and the best the player gets is to endure it.`

### Gothic / Haunting
- **Name:** Gothic / Haunting
- **One-line pitch:** A place and its past haunt you; the dread is atmosphere and the truth is buried in the house.
- **Core loop:** inhabit an oppressive place, uncover its buried past, and confront the wrong that haunts it.
- **Dramatize:** atmosphere and the place itself — the building's mood, the residue of past wrongs, the slow disclosure of secrets, the line between the supernatural and the psychological.
- **Hand-wave:** the wider world; fast action; anything that breaks the claustrophobic mood.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming saturation — atmosphere thickens, the past presses in, toward a reckoning.
- **Win condition:** uncover and lay to rest the haunting truth — break the cycle, free yourself or the place.
- **Loss condition:** consumed by the place/past — driven out broken, or absorbed into the haunting.
- **World requires:** an oppressive place with a buried past; secrets discoverable through inhabitation; a wrong that haunts and can be confronted; atmosphere as a load-bearing element.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — GOTHIC: Make the place a character — dramatize its mood, the residue of old wrongs, and the slow disclosure of buried secrets, keeping the line between supernatural and psychological blurred. Hand-wave the wider world and fast action. Thicken atmosphere toward a reckoning that lays the haunting to rest or consumes the player.`

## Family: Mind & Craft

### Puzzle / Lateral Problem-Solving
- **Name:** Puzzle / Problem-Solving
- **One-line pitch:** You face a closed problem and the satisfaction is cracking it.
- **Core loop:** examine a constrained problem, form and test hypotheses, and find the configuration that solves it.
- **Dramatize:** the problem's structure and the player's reasoning — the components, the constraints, the feedback on each attempt, the click of insight.
- **Hand-wave:** narrative connective tissue; anything not bearing on the problem's logic.
- **Tension shape:** stepwise toward a solve — pressure is the gap between confusion and insight; mild time/resource clocks optional.
- **Win condition:** solve it — find the configuration/answer the problem demands.
- **Loss condition:** stuck past recovery, or a clock/resource runs out before the solve.
- **World requires:** a closed problem with a real solution; legible constraints and components; honest feedback on attempts; a fair path to insight.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — PUZZLE: Render the problem's structure clearly — components, constraints, and honest feedback on every attempt — and dramatize the player's reasoning toward the click of insight. Hand-wave narrative that doesn't bear on the logic. Tension is the gap between confusion and the solve; keep the path fair.`

### Crafting / Mastery
- **Name:** Crafting / Mastery
- **One-line pitch:** You pursue skill itself, refining a craft from clumsy to virtuosic.
- **Core loop:** practice and refine a discipline, manage materials and technique, and produce work of rising quality.
- **Dramatize:** the craft and its progress — technique, materials, the difference between competent and excellent work, the visible growth of skill and reputation within the discipline.
- **Hand-wave:** unrelated plot; action; the world beyond the craft and its market.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming mastery curve with milestone challenges — each piece harder, each commission a test.
- **Win condition:** achieve mastery / produce the masterwork / earn recognition as a true practitioner.
- **Loss condition:** stagnate, ruin a defining work, or be eclipsed/discredited in the discipline.
- **World requires:** a craft with depth and a quality gradient; materials and technique that can be managed; challenges/commissions of rising difficulty; recognition that can be earned.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — CRAFTING/MASTERY: Slow down on the craft — technique, materials, and the real difference between competent and excellent work — and make the player's growing skill and reputation visible. Hand-wave unrelated plot and action. Climb a mastery curve with each piece harder than the last toward a masterwork or recognition.`

### Caretaking / Management
- **Name:** Caretaking / Management
- **One-line pitch:** You tend a living system or charge over time, keeping it healthy against steady pressures.
- **Core loop:** monitor the state of a system/charge, allocate limited attention and resources, and keep it thriving against decay and shocks.
- **Dramatize:** the state of the thing tended — its needs, its drift, the trade-offs of limited attention, the small daily decisions that compound; the slow reward of something flourishing.
- **Hand-wave:** action and grand plot; the world beyond the system's boundary.
- **Tension shape:** steady-pressure accretion with shock events — slow drift demands constant tending; crises spike.
- **Win condition:** the system/charge thrives and reaches its goal state (healthy, grown, delivered safely).
- **Loss condition:** neglect or crisis collapses it — the system fails, the charge is lost.
- **World requires:** a system or charge with tracked state and needs; limited resources/attention to allocate; ongoing decay and occasional shocks; a goal state it can reach or fail.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — CARETAKING: Track the state of the thing tended — its needs, its drift, the trade-offs of limited attention — and make small daily decisions compound. Hand-wave action and grand plot. Apply steady pressure that demands constant tending, with occasional shocks; reward the slow flourishing of something kept alive.`

## Family: Trajectory & Identity

### Redemption / Fall
- **Name:** Redemption / Fall Arc
- **One-line pitch:** The game is the moral trajectory of a self — whether you rise toward grace or slide toward ruin.
- **Core loop:** make value-laden choices under pressure, each nudging the self toward redemption or corruption, and live with the cumulative person you become.
- **Dramatize:** moral choice and its weight — temptation, the cost of doing right or wrong, how others read your changing self, the internal reckoning; the standing of the soul as a tracked trajectory.
- **Hand-wave:** plot mechanics and action except where they force a moral choice; neutral filler.
- **Tension shape:** trajectory accretion toward a defining test — small choices bank a direction; a climactic moment cashes it in.
- **Win condition:** the chosen arc completed — earned redemption, or (for a fall) a fully committed, coherent ruin; an honest moral landing.
- **Loss condition:** an arc that betrays itself — backslide that undoes the climb, or a fall the player didn't mean to take.
- **World requires:** meaningful moral choices with real costs; a self that can change and be read by others; pressures/temptations that test the trajectory; a defining moment that resolves the arc.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — REDEMPTION/FALL: Make every value-laden choice land — temptation, the cost of doing right or wrong, how others read the changing self — and treat the soul as a tracked trajectory. Hand-wave plot except where it forces a moral choice. Bank direction through small choices toward a defining test that cashes the arc in.`

### Coming-of-Age / Becoming
- **Name:** Coming-of-Age
- **One-line pitch:** You grow into a new self through formative experiences that change who you are.
- **Core loop:** meet formative experiences, make choices that shape an emerging identity, and integrate them into who you become.
- **Dramatize:** formative moments and inner change — first encounters, mentors and disillusionments, the choices that define values, the gap between who you were and who you're becoming.
- **Hand-wave:** routine that doesn't form the self; large-scale plot except as crucible.
- **Tension shape:** slow warming developmental arc with formative spikes toward a threshold of maturity.
- **Win condition:** cross the threshold — arrive at an integrated, chosen self; come into your own.
- **Loss condition:** fail to grow — arrested, broken by the crucible, or formed into someone the player didn't intend.
- **World requires:** a self at the start of formation; formative experiences and influences; choices that shape identity; a threshold/coming-into-one's-own to reach.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — COMING-OF-AGE: Dramatize formative moments and inner change — first encounters, mentors and disillusionments, the choices that set values, the gap between the old self and the emerging one. Hand-wave routine that doesn't form the self. Build a developmental arc toward a threshold where the player comes into their own.`

### Quest / Destiny
- **Name:** Quest / Destiny
- **One-line pitch:** You pursue a single great goal across trials that test whether you're worthy of it.
- **Core loop:** advance toward an overarching objective through escalating trials, gathering what's needed and proving worthy.
- **Dramatize:** the trials and the goal's gravity — each obstacle's test, the gathering of allies/means, the mounting cost, the sense of a fated culmination.
- **Hand-wave:** filler between trials; routine travel (compressed to passage and mood).
- **Tension shape:** ratchet of escalating trials toward a climactic culmination.
- **Win condition:** complete the quest — achieve the great goal at its climactic test.
- **Loss condition:** fail a decisive trial, lose the means, or fall short at the culmination.
- **World requires:** a single great objective with gravity; a sequence of escalating trials; means/allies to gather; a climactic test that resolves the quest.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — QUEST: Give each trial weight as a test of worth, dramatize the gathering of allies and means and the mounting cost, and keep a fated culmination on the horizon. Hand-wave filler and routine travel. Escalate trial by trial toward a climactic test that the player can pass or fail.`

## Merged / excluded

Near-variants folded into a card above (with the card they joined):
- **Noir** → tone on *Mystery* (and on *Conspiracy*); not a type.
- **Detective / casework** → *Investigative Procedural* (institutional) or *Mystery* (deduction), depending on whether process or reasoning is dramatized.
- **Slasher** → tonal/genre flavor of *Survival Horror*.
- **Psychological horror** → split between *Cosmic/Sanity Horror* (knowledge erodes the self) and *Gothic/Haunting* (place and past); the shared "dread without resources to fight" loop is *Survival Horror*.
- **Whodunnit vs. howcatchem (inverted mystery)** → both *Mystery*; the inversion is a structure choice, not a different loop.
- **Chase scene** → a beat inside *Action/Thriller* or *Disaster/Escape*, not a standalone type.
- **Survival craft / looter** → *Survival/Endurance* + *Base/Settlement Building*; the engagement shapes already cover it.
- **Diplomacy** → *Negotiation/Dealmaking* (one table) or *Political Maneuvering* (a board of factions over time).
- **Roguelike / extraction** → structural framing of *Dungeon Crawl* (push-your-luck descent) — a meta-structure, not a distinct loop.
- **Trading / economy sim** → if it's a system to tend, *Caretaking/Management*; if it's deals struck, *Negotiation*.
- **Sports / racing** → competition shapes covered by *Duel/Showdown* (one decisive contest) or *Tactical Combat* (positional contest); not given a separate card.
- **Tragedy / comedy / grimdark / camp** → tones; modifiers over any type, deliberately excluded.
- **Fantasy / sci-fi / western / cyberpunk / historical** → genres/settings; the orthogonal layer, deliberately excluded.
- **Epic / saga / "open-ended"** → scope or length, not engagement shape; covered by *Quest/Destiny* (single great goal) or *Open-World Sandbox* (self-directed).
