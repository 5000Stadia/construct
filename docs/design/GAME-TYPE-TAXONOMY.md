# Game-Type Cards — Engagement-Shape Taxonomy

> A **game type** is not a genre and not a tone. It is a claim about the **shape of engagement**: the core loop the player is actually playing, and therefore what the narrator **dramatizes vs. hand-waves**, how **tension** builds, and what **winning and losing** mean. It is valid only for a world whose substance can **support** that loop.
>
> Genre/setting (fantasy, sci-fi, noir, western, cyberpunk, gothic, historical, etc.) is an orthogonal layer. Tone (comedy, tragedy, grim, campy, cozy, erotic, operatic, bleak, lyrical, etc.) is a modifier. The test of a real type: it changes **what the narrator dwells on vs. skips** and **what victory means**. Anything that fails that test is merged, mapped, or listed as an overlay.
>
> **155 cards across 19 families.** The `World requires` field is a support-check: if a given fiction lacks those things, the type does not fit it. Most holodeck programs are compounds: for example, **Cyberpunk Heist + Social Drama + Political Intrigue**, or **Gothic Romance + Haunted House + Identity Mystery**.

---

## How to use these cards

Pick one **primary** card. That card tells the narrator what to slow down on, what to skip, how pressure should build, and what success means. Add one or two **secondary** cards only when they genuinely change play. Then add setting, tone, character arc, and delivery mode as separate layers.

A strong card combination sounds like: **what the player does + what the world supports + what victory means**. A weak one is only an aesthetic label.

### Common player appetites these cards satisfy

- **Deduction:** truth, clues, hidden systems, fair inference.
- **Mastery:** tactics, optimization, procedure, rules, performance.
- **Immersion:** place, culture, relationships, atmosphere, embodied routine.
- **Agency:** schemes, political leverage, reality editing, power ascent.
- **Care:** stewardship, healing, cozy routines, protection, repair.
- **Catharsis:** horror, tragedy, redemption, grief, revenge, moral decision.
- **Completion:** collections, maps, archives, field guides, long-term growth.

## Family 1 — Investigation & Epistemics

*Core of the family: not knowing, then coming to know. The fun is evidence, inference, interpretation, and the cost of truth.*


### Mystery / Whodunnit
- **Name:** Mystery / Whodunnit
- **One-line pitch:** You sift clues and conflicting testimony to expose a hidden truth.
- **Core loop:** Gather information and reason toward a concealed answer.
- **Dramatize:** clues, witness testimony, scenes of evidence, contradictions between accounts, motives, history, red herrings, and the player’s deductions.
- **Hand-wave:** travel, routine logistics, and the passage between clue locations.
- **Tension shape:** accretion — the noose tightens as facts align and suspects squirm.
- **Win condition:** name the truth or make the correct accusation, supported by what was uncovered.
- **Loss condition:** a wrong accusation, a missed clue, or the trail going cold.
- **World requires:** a concealed truth; at least two plausible suspects or explanations; discoverable evidence; at least one red herring.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — MYSTERY / WHODUNNIT: Dwell on clues, witness testimony, scenes of evidence, contradictions between accounts, motives, history, red herrings, and the player’s deductions. Compress travel, routine logistics, and the passage between clue locations. Let tension build as accretion — the noose tightens as facts align and suspects squirm. Make victory mean name the truth or make the correct accusation, supported by what was uncovered; make loss come from a wrong accusation, a missed clue, or the trail going cold.`


### Detective Procedural
- **Name:** Detective Procedural
- **One-line pitch:** You work the case by the book until it can survive scrutiny.

- **Dramatize:** chain of evidence, interrogation technique, alibis, canvassing, records, forensics, admissibility, and the moment a fact locks a theory.
- **Hand-wave:** pure flashes of genius, unrelated personal life, and travel between steps.

- **Win condition:** close the case with corroborated evidence and method intact.
- **Loss condition:** evidence is tainted, an alibi holds, a witness recants, or the deadline passes.
- **World requires:** a crime or wrong; a process with rules; corroborable evidence; suspects whose accounts can be tested.
- **Directive:** `PLAY STYLE — DETECTIVE PROCEDURAL: Dwell on chain of evidence, interrogation technique, alibis, canvassing, records, forensics, admissibility, and the moment a fact locks a theory. # Final passover — quality details retained and amplified

This section preserves the nuance that often gets lost when a taxonomy becomes a list.

## 1. The dwell / skip test is the whole taxonomy

A type is real only when it changes what narration spends time on. A mystery dwells on clues and testimony while compressing travel. A heist dwells on casing, roles, and the plan breaking while compressing the spending of the take. A romance dwells on charged subtext and vulnerability while compressing unrelated action. A management game dwells on flows and bottlenecks while compressing individual action. If two candidates dwell on the same substance and mean the same thing by victory, merge them.

## 2. Victory must change by type

The same surface genre can support many different victories:

- In **Cyberpunk Heist**, victory is getting the guarded asset out clean.
- In **Cyberpunk Conspiracy**, victory is exposing the hidden system without being erased.
- In **Cyberpunk Romance**, victory is mutual intimacy under dehumanizing pressure.
- In **Cyberpunk Stewardship**, victory is building a resilient enclave, business, network, or community.
- In **Cyberpunk Body Horror**, victory may be keeping identity intact while the body changes.

The surface word does not decide the win condition. The engagement shape does.

## 3. `World requires` is a gate, not flavor text

Use `World requires` as a hard support-check. You cannot run Romance without a partner with interiority and the ability to refuse. You cannot run Conspiracy without a hidden system that notices scrutiny. You cannot run Stealth without detection and consequences. You cannot run Dungeon Delve without room-by-room risk, reward, resource drain, and an exit. If the world lacks the required substance, choose another card or alter the world.

## 4. Tension shapes glossary

- **Accretion:** pressure grows as facts, debts, resources, or consequences accumulate.
- **Ratchet:** pressure only moves tighter until a break, reveal, confrontation, or escape.
- **Attrition / grind:** the body, morale, supplies, or trust wear down over time.
- **Slow warming:** emotional or atmospheric pressure deepens gradually.
- **Episodic spikes:** each scene, round, room, match, or encounter has its own pressure curve.
- **Spiral:** every answer or fix worsens the underlying instability.
- **Gated unlock:** each solved piece opens the next piece of play.
- **Oscillation:** advantage swings back and forth, common in sports, gambling, negotiation, and chases.
- **Countdown:** time is explicit and shrinking; delay itself is failure.
- **Stress test:** a built system is tested by a shock that reveals whether earlier decisions hold.

## 5. Near-siblings deliberately kept separate

These types share engines but not victories.

- **Mystery vs. Cosmic Horror:** both investigate. Mystery wins by naming the truth. Cosmic Horror may be destroyed by the truth.
- **Heist vs. Stealth:** both enter forbidden space. Heist is planned acquisition with roles and getaway. Stealth is tense traversal under detection.
- **Con vs. Negotiation:** both use words. Con wins when the mark willingly believes the false frame. Negotiation wins through explicit leverage, concessions, and agreement.
- **Political Intrigue vs. Social Drama:** both handle people. Intrigue is hard power and position. Social Drama is relational fallout, belonging, secrets, and intimacy.
- **Tactical Combat vs. Cinematic Action:** both fight. Tactical Combat rewards position and resource mastery. Cinematic Action rewards momentum, nerve, and spectacle.
- **Survival vs. Survival Horror:** both involve scarcity. Survival is about hostile conditions; Survival Horror adds predatory presence and dread.
- **Exploration vs. Ruin Exploration:** exploration asks what is out there. Ruin exploration asks what happened here.
- **Stewardship vs. Settlement Builder vs. Colony Simulator:** Stewardship is the broad system. Settlement Builder foregrounds place and civic identity. Colony Simulator foregrounds emergent needs, labor, and personalities.
- **Romance vs. Reconciliation:** both can involve love. Romance builds desire and intimacy. Reconciliation repairs named harm.
- **Saga vs. Rite of Passage:** both transform. Saga is a long chain toward a great goal. Rite of Passage is a threshold recognized by community, cosmos, or self.

## 6. Merge rules for common “genre words”

These are not standalone game types unless the play loop changes:

- **Noir / hardboiled / cozy mystery:** tone or social texture on Mystery, Procedural, Conspiracy, or Corruption.
- **Cyberpunk:** setting skin for Heist, Infiltration, Political Intrigue, Conspiracy, Body Horror, Power Ascent, or Social Drama.
- **Fantasy:** setting skin for almost anything: Saga, Dungeon Delve, Political Intrigue, Romance, Monster Hunt, Fairy-Tale Bargain, Stewardship, etc.
- **Sci-fi / space opera:** setting skin for Exploration, First Contact, Space Mission Control, Political Intrigue, Vehicle Combat, Anomaly Investigation, or Reality Editing.
- **Western:** setting skin for Duel, Frontier Exploration, Community Defense, Bounty Hunt, Revenge vs. Mercy, or Settlement Builder.
- **Gothic:** tone/setting on Haunted House, Romance, Psychological Horror, Identity Mystery, Family Saga, or Cursed Object.
- **Slasher:** usually Survival Horror or Chase with a relentless human predator; keep separate only if the loop centers victim pattern, final confrontation, or social suspicion.
- **Zombie:** can be Base Survival, Post-Collapse Scavenging, Siege Defense, Plague / Containment, Moral Lifeboat, or Creature Feature.
- **War:** scale-layer over Tactical Combat, Military Mission, Siege Defense, Stewardship, Political Intrigue, or Historical Witness.
- **Romantasy:** Romance plus Saga, Court Intrigue, Monster Hunt, or Fairy-Tale Bargain in a fantasy setting.
- **Superhero:** setting/power layer over Cinematic Action, Moral Dilemma Crucible, Power Ascent, Identity Exploration, Rescue / Extraction, or Social Climbing / Etiquette.

## 7. Character arcs as overlays, not always primary cards

The pasted model rightly warned that **coming-of-age, redemption, tragedy, and character arc labels** are often the wrong axis. In this expanded document they appear only where they can become the actual loop. Use them as overlays when the actual play is Heist, Romance, Exploration, or Combat. Use them as primary cards only when the narrator truly dwells on the interior process and victory/loss is defined by transformation, recognition, repair, or fall.

## 8. Delivery modes are not types

These describe how content is delivered, not what the player is doing:

- sandbox / open world
- linear campaign
- episodic anthology
- roguelike run structure
- legacy campaign structure, except when permanent accumulated consequences are the main loop
- live-action format
- solo journaling format
- co-op campaign
- procedural generation
- branching visual novel

Any delivery mode can host many engagement shapes.

## 9. Useful compound recipes

- **Escape-room thriller:** Puzzle / Escape + Trap Room + Conspiracy.
- **Starfleet holodeck diplomacy:** First Contact + Negotiation + Cultural Immersion.
- **Gothic manor romance:** Romance + Haunted House + Family Saga + Identity Mystery.
- **Space survival:** Extreme Environment Trek + Base Survival + Space Mission Control.
- **Occult detective:** Mystery + Occult Research + Cursed Object.
- **Courtly romantasy:** Romance + Political Intrigue + Fairy-Tale Bargain.
- **Cyberpunk crew campaign:** Heist + Infiltration + Social Drama + Faction Politics.
- **Cozy town sim:** Slice of Life + Farm / Homestead + Tavern / Social Hub + Community Healing.
- **Alien ruin expedition:** Derelict / Ruin Exploration + Scientific Anomaly + Language Decipherment.
- **Revolutionary tragedy:** Rebellion / Resistance + Revolution + Moral Dilemma Crucible + Tragedy / Fall.
- **Monster-as-protagonist:** Monster Perspective + Social Drama + Temptation / Corruption Horror.
- **Reality-bending mystery:** Mystery + Reality Editing + Identity / Memory Mystery.

## 11. A final holodeck selection rule

A good holodeck menu option should be phrased as an experience promise, not a bookshelf category.
